Fun Facts about the International Space Station

The space station has been home to astronauts for a really long time - since 2000! Can you imagine living in space for that long?
There are usually seven astronauts living and working on the space station. They fly around the Earth really fast - five miles per second. That's like racing around the Earth 16 times in one day. Because of this, the space station sees 16 sunrises and sunsets in a day!
Peggy Whitson is a superhero astronaut! She spent 665 days living and working in space.
Astronauts and cosmonauts (that's what we call astronauts from Russia) do cool spacewalks to fix things on the space station. It's like doing a really cool, really hard puzzle in space!
The space station has really big solar panels that help it fly. They're even bigger than a really big airplane! Can you imagine having solar panels that big in your backyard?
The international space station can connect to eight spaceships at the same time! That's like having a really big parking lot in space!
Astronauts can get to the space station really quickly - in just four hours!
There are millions and millions of lines of special code that help the space station work properly. It's like a really cool, really hard computer game that astronauts play to keep the space station flying.
